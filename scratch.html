How I Deliver Great Service at Olive Garden

This page is a practical, guest-first service system: timing, communication, energy, and teamwork. The goal is consistent, professional service that feels personal.

Motto: Match table energy Focus: speed + warmth

Service Philosophy

  • Match table energy. Mirror the guest’s vibe while staying professional.
  • Build friendly familiarity. Make guests feel special without being intrusive.
  • Take ownership of the experience. Guests should feel supported from hello to goodbye.
Goal: get into the “friend” portion of the guest’s mind by being helpful, confident, and warm.

Timing and Efficiency

  • Acknowledge fast. If they were just sat: “I’ll be right with you.”
  • Drinks first. People wait better with something to sip.
  • Consolidate trips. Use a tray and stack tasks into one run.
  • Setup early. Bread and setup reduces anxiety and improves flow.

My “One Trip” Checklist

  1. Before I leave the kitchen
    • Check table status and timing
    • Grab everything that can be combined
    • Prioritize drinks if they are waiting
  2. At the table
    • Confirm needs in one quick pass
    • Specific check back: “How’s the soup and salad?”
  3. After the pass
    • Ring in immediately
    • Plan the next trip in my head

Communication and Phrasing

  • If a guest requests “no X” and the dish doesn’t include X, avoid: “It doesn’t come with that.” Prefer: “Absolutely, I can do that for you.”
  • Subtle ownership. “I’ll check with my kitchen” signals care.
  • Specific check backs. Ask about the item they ordered, not “everything.”
  • Close clean. “Have a great night. Drive safe.”
Subtle credit: when delivering, name the requested detail naturally. Example: “Here’s your Blue Moon with an orange slice.”

Mistake Management

  • Fix fast. Speed and confidence protects the vibe.
  • Accountability when it’s clearly mine. Guests can detect fake blame-shifting.
  • Redirect tactfully otherwise. Explain without trashing the kitchen or team.
  • Limit errors. Rough rule: one noticeable mistake before the tip and mood drops.
The worst thing you can do is feel fake. Stay calm, be honest, and solve it.

Team Relationships

  • Be friends with hosts. They control flow and can help you when it’s slow.
  • Be good to the kitchen. Respect earns faster saves when you mess up.
  • Be friendly with everyone. Team energy carries into guest energy.

One Liners and Micro Scripts

Use sparingly. Deliver confidently. Stop if the table isn’t into it.

  • Dropping the check: “Sorry, my boss makes me hand these out.”
  • ID: “Sorry, you could be two kids in a trench coat.”
  • Hot plate: “Careful, this one’s hot. This one not so much, but it has a great personality.”
  • Free?: “Yep, it’s on the house. Unfortunately it’s your house.”
  • Plates: “Can I grab a few plates to make it look a bit prettier for you?”
  • Lots of questions: “Last question, I promise.”
  • Sold out: say “We’re sold out of” not “We’re out of.”
  • Price: “That’s a $2 difference. Is that okay?”
